Section 2 — The Economic Scale

Tourism Is a $81 Billion Engine

Australia's tourism industry contributes 2.9% of national GDP — more than agriculture, and comparable to the mining services sector. But this figure masks dramatic swings: the COVID-19 pandemic nearly halved tourism's contribution overnight.

Tourism GDP, 2016–17 to 2024–25

Total tourism gross domestic product ($M)

Tourism GDP plunged from $47B to $32B in 2020–21 — a 43% drop — before recovering to a record $81B by 2024–25.

Domestic vs. International Tourism GDP

GDP contribution by visitor origin — grouped bar chart

International tourists account for ~23% of tourism GDP in 2024–25, recovering from near-zero during border closures.

Tourism: More Than a Holiday — It's Australia's Livelihood

Australia's tourism industry is not merely a leisure sector. It is a vast economic ecosystem employing over 635,000 people, generating $81 billion in GDP, and underpinning communities from Cairns to Hobart.

The COVID-19 pandemic revealed how fragile — and how resilient — this ecosystem can be. The near-total collapse of international borders wiped out nearly half of tourism's economic contribution overnight. Yet domestic travellers, the unsung heroes of Australian tourism, kept the industry alive.

As international tourism surpasses pre-pandemic levels and employment reaches new records, Australia's tourism story is one of recovery, adaptation, and enduring importance to every Australian — whether they work in the industry or simply enjoy what it offers.

"Tourism is not just an industry. It is the way Australia shares itself with the world."
